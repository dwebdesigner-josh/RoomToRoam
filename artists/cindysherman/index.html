Untitled Film Still #07

Gelatin silver print

1978

Cindy Sherman created Untitled Film Still #07 in 1978 as part of her iconic Untitled Film Stills series. This series features black-and-white photographs in which Sherman portrays various female archetypes, drawing inspiration from the aesthetics of classic cinema. In Film Still #07, Sherman embodies a character that evokes the nostalgia and ambiguity of 1950s and 1960s film, blurring the lines between the real and the fictional.

Untitled Film Still #39

Gelatin silver print

1979

Untitled Film Still #39 invites viewers into an intimate yet ambiguous narrative. Captured in grainy black and white, this gelatin silver print presents a young woman in a bathroom, glimpsed through an open door, evoking a sense of voyeurism. The slightly blurred quality of the image enhances its dreamlike quality, leaving much to the viewer's imagination and interpretation. Each piece in this series explores themes of identity and the male gaze, prompting viewers to question the narratives that shape our understanding of femininity.

Untitled #117

Chromogenic color print

1984

In Untitled #117, Cindy Sherman continues her exploration of identity and the roles women occupy in society. Emerging from behind two black curtains, the artist presents herself as a contemporary femme fatale, combining glamour with a sense of unease. This striking image reflects the complex layers of femininity and the societal expectations placed upon women, inviting viewers to contemplate the dissonance between appearance and reality.

Untitled #153

Chromogenic color print

1985

In Untitled #153, Cindy Sherman captures a moment of haunting vulnerability, depicting a woman sprawled on the ground with a lifeless expression of distress. The disheveled figure, characterized by pallid skin and tousled silvery-grey hair, conveys a sense of isolation and despair. The muted, cool tones of the photograph contribute to its eerie atmosphere, while the dark earth and patches of greenery create an unsettling contrast that invites viewers to confront the often-hidden narratives of distress and disconnection that pervade our understanding of self.

Untitled #228

Chromogenic color print

1990

Untitled #228, created in 1990, is part of her acclaimed History Portraits series, where she explores themes of identity and representation by drawing inspiration from historical art, intertwining the personal and the historical into powerful visual commentary. In this striking photograph, Sherman presents herself in a dramatic pose, adorned in a red satin gown cinched with a dark blue sash. The gown accentuates her form while adding a regal quality. Behind her, printed textiles are parted to reveal an ominous darkness, enhancing the work's theatricality.

About the artist

Cindy Sherman is an acclaimed American photographer and conceptual artist known for her innovative work in exploring identity and representation. Born in 1954, Sherman gained prominence in the late 1970s with her groundbreaking series Untitled Film Stills, where she posed as various female characters, drawing inspiration from film and media. Through her self-portraits, she challenges traditional notions of femininity and the role of women in society, using costumes, makeup, and props to create a diverse range of personas. Sherman's work blurs the lines between art and commentary, inviting viewers to question the nature of identity and the impact of visual culture.


Over the decades, Sherman has expanded her artistic practice, experimenting with different mediums, including video and installation art. Her works have been exhibited in prestigious institutions worldwide, and she has received numerous awards for her contributions to contemporary art. Sherman's ability to reinvent herself and her artistic persona continually resonates with audiences, making her one of the most influential figures in modern art. Through her thought-provoking imagery, she has sparked conversations about the complexities of identity and the representation of women in visual media.
