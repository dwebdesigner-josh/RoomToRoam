Artists

Annie Leibovitz

Annie Leibovitz is an iconic American photographer renowned for her striking portraiture and work with celebrities.

Leibovitz gained fame for her distinctive style, blending intimate, often cinematic compositions with bold lighting to capture the essence of her subjects.

Throughout her career, she has photographed numerous cultural icons, from musicians to politicians, becoming one of the most influential portrait photographers of her time.

Natalia Vodianova as Alice

Archival pigment print

2003

Annie Leibovitz's 2003 photograph of Natalia Vodianova (Paris) captures the unique intersection of fashion and art. This image is part of a larger, fantastical photoshoot in which Leibovitz reimagines the tale of Alice in Wonderland, with Natalia Vodianova embodying Alice. The photograph evokes a dreamlike quality, where Vodianova’s poised elegance contrasts with the surreal backdrop, creating a powerful narrative about identity, transformation, and the influence of iconic figures in the fashion industry.

Sarah Jessica Parker, The Plaza Hotel

Archival pigment print

2005

Annie Leibovitz's 2005 portrait of Sarah Jessica Parker at the Plaza Hotel in New York City captures the actress in a glamorous setting, synonymous with her iconic association with New York and high fashion, particularly through her role in Sex and the City. This photograph epitomizes Leibovitz's style, which often blends celebrity with a sense of place and story, inviting viewers into the worlds of her subjects.

Julien d’Ys with Raquel Zimmermann

Archival pigment print

2008

Annie Leibovitz’s 2008 portrait of Julien d’Ys with Raquel Zimmermann masterfully captures the convergence of art, fashion, and persona. Set against a minimalist yet dramatic backdrop, the photograph emphasizes d’Ys’ avant-garde hairstyling, a key element that brings an otherworldly quality to the image. Zimmermann, poised and powerful, becomes the embodiment of both muse and subject, illustrating the transformative power of fashion.

Charlize Theron, Bushkill Falls

Archival pigment print

2023

Annie Leibovitz's photograph of Charlize Theron at Bushkill Falls, Pennsylvania, from 2011, captures the ethereal beauty of the Hollywood star, blending her elegance with nature’s grandeur. The shoot, which appeared in the December issue of Vogue, presents Theron in an otherworldly setting, styled as a sea goddess by Camilla Nickerson. This imagery contrasts the rugged, natural environment of Bushkill Falls with Theron's poise, making it a striking visual narrative that merges fashion with nature​.

Anne Hathaway, Brooklyn

Archival pigment print

2021

Annie Leibovitz's 2023 portrait of Anne Hathaway showcases Hathaway’s grace and timeless beauty, emphasizing her natural elegance. The soft, diffused lighting envelops her figure, drawing attention to her poised expression and the simple, classic styling that underscores her confident, effortless charm. Leibovitz captures an intimate, reflective side of Hathaway, creating a portrait that speaks to the quiet power of simplicity and understated glamour.

About the artist

Annie Leibovitz is a renowned American photographer known for her striking and evocative portraits of celebrities, artists, and cultural icons. Born in 1949, Leibovitz began her career as a staff photographer for Rolling Stone magazine, where her bold, intimate images helped define the visual style of the publication. Her ability to capture the essence of her subjects, often revealing their vulnerability or strength, has made her one of the most sought-after portrait photographers of her time. Leibovitz’s work spans decades and has appeared in prestigious magazines like Vanity Fair and Vogue, cementing her legacy in the world of photography.



Beyond her editorial work, Leibovitz is known for her groundbreaking gallery exhibitions and numerous photography books, including Annie Leibovitz: A Photographer's Life and Women. Her unique ability to tell a story through a single image has elevated her portraits to iconic status. With a distinctive blend of artistry and storytelling, Leibovitz continues to influence the world of photography, capturing the human spirit in ways that resonate deeply with viewers across generations.
